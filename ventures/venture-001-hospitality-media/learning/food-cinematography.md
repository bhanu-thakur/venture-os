# Food Cinematography — Venture 001 (Hospitality Media)

The spearhead skill for hotels & cafés: make food look irresistible on an iPhone. Each rung gets you cooking, pouring, and filming for real. Learn → do → hit a milestone clip → review.

The runtime reads each rung's **Goal / Lesson / Do / Milestone / Review**.

## Rung 1 — Light the dish
**Goal:** Make any plate look appetising with light alone.
**Lesson:** Food looks best in soft, directional light — ideally from the side or back, never flat-on from the front. Backlight makes drinks and sauces glow; a white card opposite the light fills the shadows. Kill yellow overhead kitchen lights.
**Do:**
- Place a dish next to a window with light coming from the side.
- Shoot it, then move the light to behind the dish (backlight) and shoot again.
- Add a white card / napkin on the dark side to bounce light back.
- Turn off overhead lights and compare the clip to your first one.
**Milestone:** The same dish shot in side-light and backlight, with fill — one clearly appetising clip.
**Review:** Which lighting made the food look most alive? Write your default food-lighting setup.

## Rung 2 — Angles & composition
**Goal:** Know which angle sells which dish.
**Lesson:** Flat-lay (top-down) suits spreads and round plates; the 45° angle suits height and layers; straight-on suits drinks, burgers, and stacks. Fill the frame, leave breathing room, and style one "hero" element.
**Do:**
- Shoot one dish three ways: top-down, 45°, and straight-on.
- Wipe the plate rim and reposition garnish for one clean "hero" frame.
- Get one extreme close-up of texture (cheese pull, crumb, sauce).
- Add a simple prop (cutlery, napkin, hand) for context in one shot.
**Milestone:** A 6-clip set of one dish that shows you can pick the right angle.
**Review:** Which angle best suited this dish? Note the rule for that dish type.

## Rung 3 — Motion & the hero moment
**Goal:** Capture the action that makes food reels stop the scroll.
**Lesson:** Movement sells food: steam rising, a sauce pour, a cheese pull, a sizzle, hands plating. Shoot these in slow motion (120/240fps) and get the timing by doing several takes.
**Do:**
- Film a pour (coffee, sauce, honey) in slow motion — at least 3 takes.
- Capture steam or sizzle by shooting against a dark background, backlit.
- Film a "hero" action: cheese pull, cut into something molten, or a garnish drop.
- Film hands plating or finishing the dish.
**Milestone:** Three strong slow-motion "action" clips (pour, hero moment, hands).
**Review:** Which action read most clearly in slow motion? Note what to prep before rolling next time.

## Rung 4 — The 15-second food reel
**Goal:** Cut your clips into a reel a hotel would proudly post.
**Lesson:** A food reel is a tiny story: tease → build → reveal → enjoy. Open on intrigue (steam, a detail), build with prep/pour, reveal the full dish, end on someone enjoying it. Cut to music.
**Do:**
- Pick your best 6–8 clips from Rungs 1–3.
- Order them tease → build → reveal → enjoy on a timeline.
- Cut to a trending/licence-free track, landing the reveal on a beat.
- Add a simple colour balance so whites look clean and food looks fresh.
**Milestone:** One 15s vertical food reel, graded and exported, good enough to show a hotel owner.
**Review:** Would a hotel post this? Note the weakest 2 seconds to reshoot.

## Rung 5 — On-location workflow (be fast in a real kitchen)
**Goal:** Walk into a café/hotel and leave in 60–90 minutes with a postable reel.
**Lesson:** On location you have little time and a busy kitchen. A shot list, a known lighting setup, and shooting near the best window make you fast and professional. Speed and calm win repeat clients.
**Do:**
- Write a one-page shot list and a 60-minute shoot plan before arriving.
- Scout the best natural light spot the moment you walk in.
- Shoot 3 dishes using your Rung 1–3 setups, checking each clip as you go.
- Edit a first reel within 24 hours and send it to the owner.
**Milestone:** A real (or full dress-rehearsal) on-location shoot → one delivered reel within 24h.
**Review:** Where did time leak? Tighten your shot list and setup for the next shoot.

## Rule
A rung is complete only when the milestone clip exists and is good enough to show a real hotel. This skill is the proof piece that unlocks the whole venture.
