# Closing Hotels — Venture 001 (Hospitality Media)

The soft skill that turns reels into rupees: finding, approaching, and closing hotels — built for the Indian, relationship-first, trust-deficit market. Each rung gets you talking to real people. This track runs in parallel with Food Cinematography all week — it never blocks a reel, and a reel never waits for it to reply.

The runtime reads each rung's **Goal / Lesson / Do / Milestone / Review**. Everything else (Why this matters, What to observe, Success criteria, Common mistakes, Stretch challenge, While you wait) is mentor commentary.

---

## Rung 1 — Build the target list
*Sprint Day 1, anytime · ~30 min desk work · doesn't compete with the Day 1 shoot*

**Goal:** A live list of 10–15 reachable, well-fit hotels & cafés.
**Lesson:** Closing starts with aiming. The best fits are owner-operated boutique/mid-range properties with a café or restaurant and a weak Instagram — a visible, fixable gap. You want a directly reachable decision-maker, not a gatekept GM at a chain property.
**Why this matters:** Every later rung this week depends on this list existing. A great reel with no one to send it to is a portfolio piece, not a business.
**What to observe:** As you scroll Instagram and Maps, notice the specific tell of a good-fit property: recent posts but poor lighting/composition, meaning they're already trying and would visibly benefit, versus a property with no social presence at all, meaning they may not care enough to respond.
**Do:**
- Search Instagram and Google Maps for boutique hotels/cafés in the Shimla belt.
- Add 10–15 to the Pipeline with name, type, and a note on their weak spot.
- Find the owner/manager's name and contact for at least 5 of them.
- Rank the top 3 you'd most like to work with.
**Milestone:** 10–15 qualified prospects in the Pipeline, top 3 marked.
**Success criteria:** Your top 3 each have an identifiable decision-maker name and a specific, written reason they're a fit — not just "seems nice."
**Common mistakes:** Adding large chains because they're easy to find — they're disqualified by `business/icp.md` for a reason, the relationship won't move fast enough this week; padding the list with properties that have no visible café/restaurant angle, diluting the food-led wedge.
**Stretch challenge:** Find one property with a genuinely standout weak spot (great food, terrible photos) — that's your highest-conversion-odds target; move it to rank #1.
**Review:** Are these owner-reachable and food-led? Drop any that aren't a fit.

## Rung 2 — The value-first opener
*Sprint Day 2 · ~30 min · uses Hospitality Reel #1 as proof*

**Goal:** A message that leads with a free gift, not a pitch.
**Lesson:** Cold pitches fail here. Lead with proof and a no-risk offer (one free signature reel). Frame it in their language — bookings and a stronger Instagram — never "videography" or "deliverables." Short, warm, personal.
**Why this matters:** Reel #1 exists now (Day 1). It's rough, and that's fine — its job today isn't to be perfect, it's to be proof that you're real and active, not a stranger making promises.
**What to observe:** Reread your draft message and count how many times it mentions you versus them. A value-first opener should be weighted toward their business, not your service.
**Do:**
- Write your opener from the template in `execution/outreach.md` — make it personal to each property's specific weak spot from Rung 1.
- Attach your best spec reel (Reel #1, or Reel #2 once it exists, whichever is stronger).
- Send it to your top 3 prospects on WhatsApp / Instagram.
- Log each send and a follow-up date in the Pipeline.
**Milestone:** 3 personalised value-first messages sent, with proof attached.
**Success criteria:** Each message references something specific about that property (their dish, their view, their weak Instagram) — not a copy-pasted identical line to all three.
**Common mistakes:** Sending the exact same generic message to all three prospects, which reads as a mass blast even when it isn't one; leading with your equipment or experience instead of their opportunity.
**While you wait:** No reply yet doesn't mean stop — move on to today's Cinematic Shooting reps or a reference study session (see `NEXT.md`). The next message to a new prospect is also always available as a productive use of waiting time.
**Review:** Any replies? Note which wording got a response and reuse it.

## Rung 3 — The in-person / call approach
*Sprint Day 3 · ~30 min plus travel*

**Goal:** Get comfortable opening the conversation face-to-face.
**Lesson:** Hill-station business runs on relationships. A calm in-person visit or call beats endless DMs. Show the reel on your phone, offer the free shoot, read their interest, and leave the door open. You're being useful, not selling.
**Why this matters:** Digital messages from Rung 2 open doors slowly in a trust-deficit market; a real conversation opens them fast. This rung is often where the actual unlock happens, not the DM.
**What to observe:** Notice your own body language and pace when you rehearse out loud — rushed delivery reads as nervous selling, even with good words. Slow down more than feels natural.
**Do:**
- Rehearse your 20-second in-person opener out loud 5 times.
- Visit or call 2 prospects; show the reel and offer one free signature reel.
- Ask one question about their current bookings/marketing and just listen.
- Note their reaction and agree a next step before leaving.
**Milestone:** 2 real in-person/phone conversations with a hotel decision-maker.
**Success criteria:** Both conversations end with a concrete next step agreed (a date, a follow-up call, a yes/no) — not a vague "let me think about it" with nothing scheduled.
**Common mistakes:** Filling silence by over-explaining instead of letting them respond — after your offer, stop talking; treating a soft "maybe" as a dead end instead of agreeing a specific follow-up date.
**While you wait:** Between visits, or if a prospect asks you to come back later, that gap is exactly when to run a "while you wait" task from `NEXT.md` — a reference study or a re-cut rep, not idle scrolling.
**Review:** Where did you freeze or oversell? Write the one line to fix your opener.

## Rung 4 — Free-reel to paid (handle the money talk)
*Sprint Day 4 · ~30 min · uses Hospitality Reel #2 (signature)*

**Goal:** Turn a happy free-reel reaction into a priced offer without flinching.
**Lesson:** Once they've seen value, present the ladder (Starter → Signature → Retainer) calmly and anchor with the package you want them on. Expect price resistance — answer with ROI (bookings, guest experience), not discounts. Silence after stating a price is your friend — the first person to speak after a number is said usually loses leverage.
**Why this matters:** Reel #2 (Deliverable #3) was built today specifically to be sent as this free gift. The craft and the business move are the same action — that's the whole point of this venture's structure.
**What to observe:** Watch your own instinct in the rehearsal — most beginners discount the instant they hear hesitation. Notice the urge and don't act on it.
**Do:**
- Deliver Reel #2 (your free signature reel) to one prospect and watch their reaction.
- Present the offer ladder and pricing from `business/offer.md` out loud.
- Practise answering "it's expensive" with a value/ROI response, not a discount.
- State a price and then stay silent — let them respond first.
**Milestone:** One real pricing conversation where you presented a package and held your price.
**Success criteria:** You stated a real price out loud to a real prospect and did not discount it within the same conversation, regardless of the outcome.
**Common mistakes:** Discounting before they've even objected, out of pre-emptive anxiety; answering "it's expensive" with a justification of your costs instead of their ROI (bookings, guest perception) — the Constitution's Desi Mindset Filter exists exactly for this moment: price sensitivity is real, so lead with paisa vasool, not with a sob story about your time.
**While you wait:** If this prospect needs a few days to decide, that's normal in this market — keep moving down your Rung 1 list rather than stalling the whole venture on one conversation.
**Review:** Which objection caught you out? Script a better answer for it.

## Rung 5 — Close & collect the advance
*Sprint Day 5 onward, whenever a prospect is warm enough · ~20 min when it happens*

**Goal:** Convert a yes into a confirmed booking with money down.
**Lesson:** In a low-trust market, a modest advance (30–50%) after they've seen the free reel both confirms commitment and protects you. Make saying yes easy: clear scope, simple terms, a near-term date. A cleared advance — not praise — is the proof the venture works, per the Constitution's Proof of Execution standard.
**Why this matters:** This is the rung the entire week's craft and outreach has been building toward. Everything from Rung 1's list to Reel 2's quality exists to make this single conversation easier.
**What to observe:** Notice the temptation to treat a warm "yes, I'm interested" as the finish line. It isn't — the booking isn't real until a date and an advance are both confirmed.
**Do:**
- Write a one-line scope + price + date for a real prospect.
- Ask directly for the booking and a 30–50% advance to lock the date.
- Offer a simple payment method (UPI) to remove friction.
- Record the outcome and amount in the Pipeline and capture the win.
**Milestone:** First confirmed booking with a cleared advance (Proof of Execution).
**Success criteria:** Money has actually moved — a confirmed verbal yes with no advance does not satisfy this milestone, by design.
**Common mistakes:** Accepting a verbal commitment as final and not asking for the advance in the same conversation, which lets momentum die; over-asking on advance percentage for a first-time client relationship, which reintroduces the trust friction the free reel was designed to remove.
**While you wait:** If no prospect is warm enough for this conversation by Day 5, this rung simply isn't ready yet this week — that's a real outcome, not a failure. Keep Rung 4 conversations moving with the rest of your list, and treat any spare time as a "while you wait" rep from `NEXT.md` (re-cutting, re-grading, more reference study) rather than forcing a close that isn't there.
**Review:** What finally tipped the yes? Bank the lesson for the next close.
