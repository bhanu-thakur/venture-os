# Closing Hotels — Venture 001 (Hospitality Media)

The soft skill that turns reels into rupees: finding, approaching, and closing hotels — built for the Indian, relationship-first, trust-deficit market. Each rung gets you talking to real people. Learn → do → hit a milestone → review.

The runtime reads each rung's **Goal / Lesson / Do / Milestone / Review**.

## Rung 1 — Build the target list
**Goal:** A live list of 10–15 reachable, well-fit hotels & cafés.
**Lesson:** Closing starts with aiming. The best fits are owner-operated boutique/mid-range properties with a café or restaurant and a weak Instagram — a visible, fixable gap. You want a directly reachable decision-maker.
**Do:**
- Search Instagram and Google Maps for boutique hotels/cafés in the Shimla belt.
- Add 10–15 to the Pipeline with name, type, and a note on their weak spot.
- Find the owner/manager's name and contact for at least 5 of them.
- Rank the top 3 you'd most like to work with.
**Milestone:** 10–15 qualified prospects in the Pipeline, top 3 marked.
**Review:** Are these owner-reachable and food-led? Drop any that aren't a fit.

## Rung 2 — The value-first opener
**Goal:** A message that leads with a free gift, not a pitch.
**Lesson:** Cold pitches fail here. Lead with proof and a no-risk offer (one free signature reel). Frame it in their language — bookings and a stronger Instagram — never "videography" or "deliverables." Short, warm, personal.
**Do:**
- Write your opener from the template in `execution/outreach.md` — make it personal.
- Attach your best spec reel (your Food Cinematography milestone).
- Send it to your top 3 prospects on WhatsApp / Instagram.
- Log each send and a follow-up date in the Pipeline.
**Milestone:** 3 personalised value-first messages sent, with proof attached.
**Review:** Any replies? Note which wording got a response and reuse it.

## Rung 3 — The in-person / call approach
**Goal:** Get comfortable opening the conversation face-to-face.
**Lesson:** Hill-station business runs on relationships. A calm in-person visit or call beats endless DMs. Show the reel on your phone, offer the free shoot, read their interest, and leave the door open. You're being useful, not selling.
**Do:**
- Rehearse your 20-second in-person opener out loud 5 times.
- Visit or call 2 prospects; show the reel and offer one free signature reel.
- Ask one question about their current bookings/marketing and just listen.
- Note their reaction and agree a next step before leaving.
**Milestone:** 2 real in-person/phone conversations with a hotel decision-maker.
**Review:** Where did you freeze or oversell? Write the one line to fix your opener.

## Rung 4 — Free-reel to paid (handle the money talk)
**Goal:** Turn a happy free-reel reaction into a priced offer without flinching.
**Lesson:** Once they've seen value, present the ladder (Starter → Signature → Retainer) calmly and anchor with the package you want them on. Expect price resistance — answer with ROI (bookings, guest experience), not discounts. Silence after stating a price is your friend.
**Do:**
- Deliver a free signature reel to one prospect and watch their reaction.
- Present the offer ladder and pricing from `business/offer.md` out loud.
- Practise answering "it's expensive" with a value/ROI response, not a discount.
- State a price and then stay silent — let them respond first.
**Milestone:** One real pricing conversation where you presented a package and held your price.
**Review:** Which objection caught you out? Script a better answer for it.

## Rung 5 — Close & collect the advance
**Goal:** Convert a yes into a confirmed booking with money down.
**Lesson:** In a low-trust market, a modest advance (30–50%) after they've seen the free reel both confirms commitment and protects you. Make saying yes easy: clear scope, simple terms, a near-term date. A cleared advance — not praise — is the proof the venture works.
**Do:**
- Write a one-line scope + price + date for a real prospect.
- Ask directly for the booking and a 30–50% advance to lock the date.
- Offer a simple payment method (UPI) to remove friction.
- Record the outcome and amount in the Pipeline and capture the win.
**Milestone:** First confirmed booking with a cleared advance (Proof of Execution).
**Review:** What finally tipped the yes? Bank the lesson for the next close.

## Rule
A rung is complete only when the real-world action happened — a message sent, a conversation had, an advance cleared. You cannot practise closing in your head.
