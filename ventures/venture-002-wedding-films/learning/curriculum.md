# Cinematic Editing — Venture 002 (Wedding Films)

A skill ladder you *climb by doing*. Each rung is one loop: learn a little → take real actions → hit a milestone you can show → review. You advance only when the milestone is finished and shown to one real person.

The runtime reads each rung's **Goal / Lesson / Do / Milestone / Review**. The **Do** items are real-world actions — get them done, check them off, and the milestone unlocks.

## Rung 1 — Footage flow (organize so you can create)
**Goal:** Turn a pile of clips into a clean timeline you can actually edit from.
**Lesson:** Editors lose hours to chaos. Before any art, build a system: import, label, and select your best shots. A small set of great clips beats a hard drive of mediocre ones.
**Do:**
- Create a new DaVinci project named with the date and "practice".
- Import one batch of footage (your own or free wedding b-roll from Pexels/Mixkit).
- Watch every clip once and flag your 10 best moments only.
- Drop those 10 onto the timeline in story order: arrival → details → people → emotion.
**Milestone:** A 60–90s rough "selects" timeline of your 10 best shots, exported.
**Review:** Watch it back. Do the 10 shots already tell a tiny story? Note the one weak shot to cut next time.

## Rung 2 — The cut (rhythm & story)
**Goal:** Control how an edit *feels* through cut length and order.
**Lesson:** Every wedding edit is four beats — establish, detail, emotion, payoff. Long holds breathe; fast cuts lift energy. Cut on emotion, not on action.
**Do:**
- Pick one reference film from `references.md` and watch its first 30s three times.
- Count every cut and mark where the music turns — write the shot lengths down.
- Rebuild that timing with your own selects (match the rhythm, not the footage).
- Trim every shot by 10% and feel how the pace tightens.
**Milestone:** One 20–30s sequence whose pacing matches the reference, exported.
**Review:** Play yours and the reference back to back. Where does yours drag or rush? One fix for next time.

## Rung 3 — Music & sync
**Goal:** Make music drive emotion instead of just playing underneath.
**Lesson:** Music is the spine. Cut major moments onto beats; use J-cuts (sound leads in) and L-cuts (sound lingers out). A held breath of silence before a swell is a tool.
**Do:**
- Choose one track and mark its beat grid and its single biggest swell.
- Place your strongest visual moment exactly on that swell.
- Land every hard cut on a beat for one 45–60s stretch.
- Add one J-cut and one L-cut so audio crosses the edit.
**Milestone:** A 45–60s beat-synced cut where every major cut feels "on time."
**Review:** Watch once with eyes closed — does the sound alone carry the arc? Note one cut that fights the music.

## Rung 4 — Colour (the cinematic look)
**Goal:** Give footage a deliberate, consistent mood — and be able to reuse it.
**Lesson:** Grade in order: balance (neutral, correct) → contrast (depth) → look (mood). One consistent look beats ten clever ones, and a saved look makes you fast.
**Do:**
- On the colour page, balance exposure and white on every shot of your Rung 3 piece.
- Add contrast with the curves until it has depth.
- Apply one deliberate look (warm filmic or soft teal-orange).
- Save it as a reusable power-grade / LUT.
**Milestone:** Your Rung 3 piece, fully graded, plus one saved look you can reapply.
**Review:** Toggle the grade on/off. Does it add feeling or just colour? Keep or kill it in one sentence.

## Rung 5 — Story across a full film
**Goal:** Hold attention across a 2–3 minute highlight with a real arc.
**Lesson:** A highlight is a small movie: a hook in the first 10s, rising tension, a release, and a callback to the opening at the end. Structure first, pretty shots second.
**Do:**
- Write the five beats on paper: hook → build → peak → resolve → callback.
- Assemble each beat as its own mini-section before fine-cutting.
- Place your single best moment at the peak.
- Echo your opening shot at the very end (the callback).
**Milestone:** One 2–3 minute highlight edit with a clear arc and a callback ending.
**Review:** Show it to one person who wasn't there. Did they feel the arc? Mark where attention dropped.

## Rung 6 — Signature & speed (paid-ready)
**Goal:** Edit fast, in a style that's recognisably yours — ready to take money.
**Lesson:** Professionals are recognisable and fast. A signature makes you memorable; templates and presets make you profitable. Speed turns skill into income.
**Do:**
- Build a project template with your saved looks, titles, and bins.
- Edit one more highlight using only the template, and time yourself.
- Write one paragraph defining what makes an edit recognisably *yours*.
- Post the finished piece publicly (Instagram/Vimeo) as portfolio.
**Milestone:** A portfolio-grade highlight posted publicly + a one-page documented workflow (promote to `library/` once it's worked twice).
**Review:** Compare this edit's time and quality to Rung 5's. Faster and better? You're ready for a paid edit or a second-shooter slot.

## Rule
Never skip the milestone. A rung is complete only when something is finished and shown to one real person. Finished work is the only proof the skill moved.
