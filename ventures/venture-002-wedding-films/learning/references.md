# Reference Films — Venture 002 (Wedding Films)

Films to study by **reconstruction**, not admiration. For each: watch once for feeling, then break down the first 30s shot-by-shot and rebuild the rhythm in DaVinci.

## How to study a reference
1. Watch once, full screen, no notes — just feel where it moves you.
2. Watch again counting cuts: how long is each shot? where does music turn?
3. Note the **opening 3 shots** and the **first emotional beat**.
4. Rebuild that sequence's *timing* with your own / stock footage.

## Where to find references
- **YouTube / Vimeo:** search "cinematic wedding film", "wedding teaser", top Indian wedding filmmakers (e.g. high-end teasers — study the editing, not the budget).
- **Instagram Reels:** "#weddingfilm", "#cinematicwedding" — short-form pacing is closest to what you'll sell first.
- **Bollywood / Hollywood:** title sequences and montage scenes — the storytelling bar you're aiming for.
